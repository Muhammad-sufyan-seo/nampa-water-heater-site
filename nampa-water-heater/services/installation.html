Water Heater Installation Services in Nampa, ID

A water heater installation is one of those jobs where cutting corners costs you twice. An undersized unit means cold showers. Improper venting creates carbon monoxide risk. Missing permits can void your homeowner's insurance and complicate a future home sale. Types of Water Heater Installation We Offer

Tank Water Heater Installation

Traditional tank water heaters remain the most common choice for Nampa homeowners—they're reliable, cost-effective upfront, and straightforward to install. We install gas and electric tank units in 30, 40, 50, and 75+ gallon configurations. For most Nampa households, a 40–50 gallon unit handles a family of four comfortably.

When sizing a new tank, we account for your household's peak demand, not just average use. A family that showers in the morning before school and work needs a unit sized for that simultaneous demand—not a unit that technically "covers" the average daily gallon count.

Tankless Water Heater Installation

Tankless (on-demand) water heaters heat water only when you need it, eliminating standby heat loss and providing an essentially unlimited supply of hot water. They're a popular upgrade in South Nampa's newer and luxury homes, where homeowners are investing in long-term efficiency.

Tankless installation in Nampa requires careful attention to two factors specific to this area:

  • Hard water descaling: Nampa's 200–350 ppm water hardness will scale a tankless heat exchanger faster than the manufacturer's maintenance schedule assumes. We recommend a water softener or inline descaler alongside any tankless installation here.
  • Gas line sizing: Most tankless gas units require a larger gas line than a traditional tank. We assess your existing supply line and upgrade it if needed—this is not optional and is often overlooked by less experienced installers.

We install Navien, Rinnai, Noritz, Rheem, and A.O. Smith tankless units for both residential and light commercial applications.

Heat Pump (Hybrid) Water Heater Installation

Heat pump water heaters use electricity to move heat from the surrounding air into the water rather than generating heat directly. They're 2–3x more efficient than standard electric resistance water heaters and qualify for federal tax credits under the Inflation Reduction Act (up to 30% of installed cost, up to $600).

They work best in unconditioned or semi-conditioned spaces (garages, utility rooms) where ambient air temperature stays above 40°F. Nampa's climate makes them viable year-round in most installations.

Idaho Code Requirements for Water Heater Installation

Idaho follows the International Residential Code (IRC) for residential water heater installations. Key requirements include:

  • Seismic strapping: Required per IRC to prevent tip-over during seismic events.
  • Pressure relief valve (T&P valve): Required on all tank water heaters, with a discharge pipe routed to within 6 inches of the floor.
  • Expansion tank: Required in closed plumbing systems (common in newer Nampa homes with backflow preventers).
  • Venting: Gas water heaters require proper Category I or Category III venting per the appliance type and installation location.
  • Permits: A plumbing permit is required for all water heater installations in Canyon County.
Why Permits Matter An unpermitted water heater installation can void your homeowner's insurance coverage for water damage, complicate a future home sale, and leave you liable if the installation causes injury or property damage. We pull all required permits on every job.
